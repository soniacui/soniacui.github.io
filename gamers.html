Background

The Problem

With everyone staying at home due to the unfortunate COVID-19, gaming has become more popular than ever as a means to pass time and bond with others. However, it can be difficult to find people to play games with. Friends aren't always free, or want to play the same games as you, or the same skill level as you.

The Goal

Create an efficient and practical way to help people to meet others through games.


Research

Primary Research

I conducted surveys and interviews to dive deeper into understanding how gamers currently look for gaming companions, what exactly they look for, and what they find difficult about the process.

Key Insights

The main patterns I noticed among the feedback were:

  1. People mainly find people to play games with through Discord, current friends, or mutual friends.
  2. The most important factors when looking for someone to play games with are availability, shared interests, and skill level.
  3. The main obstacles to finding people to play games with are differing schedules, skill levels, or differing intentions with the game (e.g. casual vs. serious).
Pain Points

Among the survey and interview feedback, these were the top 3 concerns I discovered:


  1. People have differing schedules

    Everybody's busy with something, and can't always play at a moment's notice. People often have different sleep schedules, or have school/work deadlines. It's especially to sync up a good time for games that require more people, such as Among Us. This was the #1 complaint about finding people to play with.

  2. Friends may have different skill levels

    Even if a few friends are free at the same time and both love the same game, they might not be able to play well together. For example, in League of Legends, a person ranked very highly at Diamond cannot play ranked with someone ranked much lower at Bronze. They could play casually, but the skill gap may make the game feel too easy or too difficult for either player.

  3. Interests and personalities need to align

    Friends might not play the same games, or may have different goals within the game (e.g. casual vs. serious). For example, some people don't play shooters because it makes them dizzy, some people only play adventure games, or some people only want to play ranked.

Opportunities

What can we take away from scouting out the competition?


  1. Time Scheduling

    Most solutions currently don't have a robust time scheduling tool - this can combat differing schedules because it will allow users to make time for events and communicate beforehand when they're free. Since this was the #1 obstacle to finding people to play games with, I'll make this a priority.

  2. Go for High Reach

    Most solutions currently have low reach - how can we create a practical solution with high reach to connect the maximum number of users?

  3. Filtering By Interests

    It takes time to get to know someone and understand whether you have the same game interests - how can we filter people with differing interests beforehand?

Design

The Concept: And Beyond

A discord extension that allows users to find public servers, filtered by interest.

Justification

Discord has over 100 million regular users - most gamers already use Discord. I want to take advantage of its large user base to build on top of what it already does well.


Traditionally, Discord servers are private and require and invitation to join. However, this is a waste of potential for servers that are meant to be discovered. For example, Toronto Pokemon Go servers, Among Us servers, and many more are constantly ushering in more people!


And Beyond is a discord extension that will allow users to search beyond their private servers to discover new gaming communities that they click with!

Key Features

Search by game

Searching by game will help narrow down the server results - most users will have a few games in mind that they want to play.

Narrow by interests/skill level

The #2 obstacle to finding people to play games with was differing interests or skill level within the game (e.g. casual vs. serious). After filtering for the game they want, the user can choose among various servers depending on game interest/goal, and see mutuals already in the server.


Events channel to vote for availability

The #1 obstacle to finding people to play games with was availability. With this extension, servers will have a built-in 'events' channel, in which people can vote for a time that they're free to meet up. Furthermore, the event time will be scheduled ahead of time, so people will be more likely to make time for it.

Constraints and Style Guide

Since I'm creating a Discord extension, I want to make sure my tone and design match Discord's. The typeface I chose is Segoe UI, which is identical to Discord's actual font. I made sure to match Discord's casual tone and dark colour palette throughout designing.

Reflections

What I Learned

  1. Keep user needs in mind

    Throughout the design process, I found myself wondering how I should design the functionality of various features. My automatic reaction was to think, "What do I want?", but I know that I'm not designing for myself. I learned to keep research feedback at the front of my mind when designing, and ask for other people's feedback when stuck.

  2. Stay aware of biases

    Looking back, there was a bit of bias in my survey questions. For example, asking 'How easy is it for you to find people to play games with?' may imply that the process is supposed to be easy. Furthermore, giving users limited answers to choose from may only give me parts of the whole story. Next time, I'll be more wary of potential biases when designing user surveys.

For the future...

  1. Edit UI for 'vote for availability' dropdown

    The dropdown is currently clear and does its job, but I think it could be improved to look more cohesive with the rest of the app.

  2. Test and iterate more

    I can't read people's minds, so I should conduct some usability tests and collect feedback for the final designs of my product. I tested with two sample users, but I should collect more information for more complete feedback and interate with improvements.
