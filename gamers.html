The Problem

With everyone staying at home due to the unfortunate COVID-19, gaming has become more popular than ever as a means to pass time and bond with others. However, it can be difficult to find people to play games with. Friends aren't always free, or want to play the same games as you, or the same skill level as you.

Goal

Create an efficient and practical way to help people to meet others through games


Research

Primary Research

I conducted surveys and interviews to dive deeper into understanding how gamers currently look for gaming companions, what exactly they look for, and what they find difficult about the process.

Insights
  1. People mainly find people to play games with through Discord, current friends, or mutual friends
  2. The most important factors when looking for someone to play games with are availability, shared interests, and skill level
  3. The main obstacles to finding people to play games with are differing schedules, skill levels, or differing intentions with the game (e.g. casual vs. serious)

Pain Points

  1. People have differing schedules
    Everybody's busy with something, and can't always play at a moment's notice. People often have different sleep schedules, or have school/work deadlines. It's especially to sync up a good time for games that require more people, such as Among Us. This was the #1 complaint about finding people to play with.
  2. Friends may have different skill levels
    Even if a few friends are free at the same time and both love the same game, they might not be able to play well together. For example, in League of Legends, a person ranked very highly at Diamond cannot play ranked with someone ranked much lower at Bronze. They could play casually, but the skill gap may make the game feel too easy or too difficult for either player.
  3. Interests and personalities need to align
    Friends might not play the same games, or may have different goals within the game (e.g. casual vs. serious). For example, some people don't play shooters because it makes them dizzy, some people only play adventure games, or some people only want to play ranked.

Opportunities

People want to play with friends, mutuals, or people with shared interests so they'll get along. → Can form a network of mutuals and gather people together based on an already-shared interest. People have differing schedules → Create simple scheduling so that people can sign up for events at a certain time and date; people can vote for their desired time and make time in advance for the event. People like playing different games, or have different goals within a game (casual vs. serious) → Group people based on the game they're interested in and their intentions within the game

The Redesign

The Concept

A Discord extension that allows people to search for public servers - they can search by game and see mutuals already in the server. There will also be a new 'Events' type channel in each server to create events. #1 complaint was availability -> vote for meetups ahead of time so people can easily decide a time, and make time for it #2 complaint was similar personality/intentions w the game -> people can join game-specific servers that match their goals (e.g. diamond+, sweaty, casual, etc...) #3 complaint was skill level -> people can join game-specific servers that meet their skill level (e.g. diamond+, plat+, ...) I decided to with concept 2 because it seemed more practical - 89% of gamers already use Discord, so it would be smarter to build on top of something that people already use. I can take advantage of the fact that people connect through already-existing mutuals on Discord, and that they're already used to the UI. Traditionally, Discord servers are private and require and invitation to join. However, there are thousands of servers meant to have as many people as possible, such Toronto Pokemon Go servers, Among Us servers, and many more... And Beyond is a discord extension that will allow users to search beyond their the users' private servers to discover public servers for the games and people they desire! Great way to find people to play games with & meet new people

Wireframes

Constraints

Since I'm creating a Discord extension, I need to make sure my tone and design match Discord's. I chose typeface Segoe UI, identical to Discord's own font, and followed their colour palette and tone throughout the design process.

Prototypes

Here are the final designs of Discord and Beyond.

Features

1. Search for public server by game; can see/filter by tags if looking for a game
2. Search for server by style/intent with the game/rank, e.g. casual/competitive, diamond+, main champion, location (EU), ...)
3. Public servers have specific EVENT channels to make it easier to host events Like a regular text channel to discuss the upcoming event, but also a poll that allows users to vote for the event time to maximize the # of people who can come - availability was the #1 complaint in user surveys.
