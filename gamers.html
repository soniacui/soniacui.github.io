The Problem

With everyone staying at home due to the unpredented COVID-19, gaming has become more popular than ever, especially as a tool to pass time and bond with others. Video game streamers on twitch views have gone up 50% in just between April and May of 2020. As more and more people turn to gaming for entertainment, I wanted to tackle the issue of wanting to play a super fun game, but not having the people to play it with/wanting to bond with people over games.

Goal

To make it easier for people to find people to play games with/meet people through games.


Research

Primary Research

I conducted surveys and interviews to dive deeper into what gamers find important in gaming friends. I discovered that:

  • people mainly find people to play games with through Discord, or current/mutual friends
  • the most important factors when looking for someone to play games with are: availability, shared interests, and skill level
  • the main obstacles to finding people to play games with are differing schedules, skill levels, or differing intentions with the game (e.g. casual vs. serious)

Pain Points

  1. People generally don't want to play with strangers and would rather play with friends or mutuals
  2. Sometimes friends don't play the same games or have different goals with the game
  3. People have different schedules, so it's sometimes difficult to sync up a good time to play together

Key Takeaways ##### Opportunities
People want to play with friends, mutuals, or people with shared interests so they'll get along. → Can form a network of mutuals and gather people together based on an already-shared interest.
People have differing schedules → Create simple scheduling so that people can sign up for events at a certain time and date; people can vote for their desired time and make time in advance for the event.
People like playing different games, or have different goals within a game (casual vs. serious) → Group people based on the game they're interested in and their intentions within the game
